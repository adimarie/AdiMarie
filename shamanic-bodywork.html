Somatic Healing

Shamanic Bodywork

The body as altar. Sensation as oracle.

The Offering

Where the flesh
meets the sacred

The living flesh and clay of your body vessel is the keeper of your many truths. Every wound you have survived, every grief you have carried, every moment of profound joy — all of it lives in the body, encoded in muscle and fascia, in the rhythm of breath and the pattern of tension.

Shamanic Bodywork brings together somatic therapeutic touch, shamanic energy medicine, and ceremonial awareness to create a session that works simultaneously on the physical and energetic levels. This is not massage for relaxation — though deep rest often follows. This is bodywork as ceremony: intentional, held, and oriented toward the restoration of wholeness.

Adi Marie brings sixteen years of hands-on practice and a highly developed somatic sensitivity to every session. She reads the body's story — in areas of holding, in the quality of tissue, in the presence or absence of aliveness in different regions. From this reading, she moves — with skilled hands and tuned awareness — to release what has been held and restore the sacred conversation between flesh, spirit, and land.

The body knows everything the mind is still figuring out. This work creates the conditions for the body to speak — and for you to finally hear it. — Adi Marie

Sessions are available in person in Topanga Canyon, Los Angeles. Distance energetic sessions are also available for those unable to travel. Each session is approximately 2 hours and includes a brief integration conversation at the close.

What to Expect

◈

A brief intake conversation to understand what you're carrying and what you're seeking — not a clinical intake, but a sacred one.

◈

A ceremonial opening — setting sacred space, calling in the directions and elements, establishing the container for the work.

◈

The bodywork itself — a weaving of deep tissue technique, energy medicine, and somatic awareness, guided by what the body is offering and asking for.

◈

A ceremonial close and brief integration conversation — space to reflect on what arose and how to tend it going forward.
